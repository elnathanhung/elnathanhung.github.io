“Nostalgia Reworked” is a speculative design for an exhibition installation that mixes elements of nostalgia with expressions of the contemporary. The project was designed to be situated within the Nanyang Technological University, School of Art, Design and Media Gallery (ADM Gallery), for an exhibition with the theme of “Singapore Dubbing”. This theme is inspired by the notion of Dub music; the blending of two ‘genres’ of spaces. Within the exhibition model, we see the dubbing of two points of view within the space; on one side we see visual elements from olden-day Singapore (e.g. shophouse windows), while on the other, we see hints of modern design (e.g. the Bauhaus Globe chair). As a result, the viewers walk into a space not exactly knowing which point in time they have entered.

The exhibition installation includes a soundscape demonstrating the different reminiscences triggered by the nostalgic objects found. The video demonstration is a prototype solution for a speculative interactive component designed for the soundscape. Visitors take on the role of 'the conductor' as they have control over the atmospheric landscape. I have selected a total of three nostalgic elements. The first is a radio which corresponds to the audio broadcast of an old Singaporean tune ("Bengawang Solo" by The Sundowners). Next, is one retro television, which triggers the sound of a Singaporean TV broadcast 50 years ago. Lastly, we have a traditional round marble table, which activates the sound from roadsides of old coffee shops (or 'kopitiams') in the early morning. Through the gesture of pointing from one object to the next, visitors within the space can select and tune in to their desired soundscape. This project has yet to explore other means of interaction within the space. The prototype was programmed with Mediapipe on Python, Wekinator (for data training), and TouchDesigner.
