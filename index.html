“Monkey See, Monkey Do” refers to the act of imitation with the limited knowledge of consequences or reason. Playing on the idea that a small ripple can create a tsunami, Whackinator serves as a commentary on society need for inclusion and the hive mind mentality. The hands are sets of servo motors programmed with Raspberry Pi computers while the hand sensing is through Python Mediapipe which communicates to the servos via OSC message.

Paya Lebar Airbase: the place to be… but we can’t is an interactive website entailing history and personal encounters within the location, Paya Lebar Airbase (PLAB) in Singapore. As usual protocols for high security military airbases would suggest, PLAB is blocked from the public eye apart from open house event coverages and images posted on the internet (which are mostly given security clearance). Through this interactive narrative, I experiment with how one can experience a place where one cannot enter or see.

This project pushes the boundaries of what we can find or document about a confidential location. The entire website is developed and coded from scratch. Chapter 1 documents some of the history of PLAB, chapter 2 is an imagining of the experience of the location as I narrate my own personal experiences within the base, and lastly, chapter 3 is an interactive activity where I invite you to plan the future of PLAB.

The goal of the work was to remake the Graffiti Research Lab laser graffiti project. I used TouchDesigner to recreate the graffiti through blob tracking and projection. The colours of the strokes are defined by a “noise operator”, resulting in a vibrant visual effect. Opencv allows us to map the webcam image input according to the projection output. The input image is distorted to fit the dimensions of the projection using a “stoner palette”.

The Day Before choose your own adventure narrative inspired by the film "Primer" (2004). This game was created with TouchDesigner using containers and buttons which form a basic user interface with selected paths. Imagine you are a broke inventor who is in desperate to win the lottery. Your only way to win is to lose first, travel back to the day before and submit the winning numbers. Things start to go wrong.

The Terrain Project is an visual sound-waves that response to audio signals done with TouchDesigner. These signals can be sourced from either an external microphone or instrument, which provide live input signals (or “Live In”), or signals from an internally stored playlist, mp3 tracks or movie files that are saved within the project directory. The signal to be channeled into the programming environment is analysed as 3 different patterns: high, middle, and low frequencies. These are translated into waves which are visualised as columns in a grid. The result is a moving three-dimensional oscilloscope (drawn in x, y, z axes) containing wave-like qualities.
